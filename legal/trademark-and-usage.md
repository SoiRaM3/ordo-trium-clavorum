# Trademark and Usage

## Purpose

This document defines the permitted use of the name, symbols, and identifying materials of the **Order of the Three Nails** (*Ordo Equitum Trium Clavorum*).

Its purpose is to prevent misuse, misrepresentation, and symbolic inflation.

---

## Name and Titles

The names:

* Order of the Three Nails
* Ordo Equitum Trium Clavorum

and all internal titles and offices are identifiers of a specific voluntary association.

They may not be used to:

* imply legal, ecclesial, or political authority
* claim endorsement or affiliation without authorization
* intimidate, influence, or mislead others

---

## Symbols and Insignia

The following are protected identifiers of the Order:

* the cross charged with three nails
* seals of the Order
* banners, badges, and official insignia
* mottos and formal legends

These may not be reproduced, altered, or displayed without explicit permission from the Order.

---

## Permitted Use

Permitted use includes:

* official documents and correspondence
* educational or scholarly reference with clear attribution
* internal devotional or ceremonial use by members

Such use must preserve the integrity and meaning of the symbols.

---

## Prohibited Use

The following uses are prohibited:

* commercial exploitation
* political advocacy or campaigning
* use in extremist, hateful, or violent contexts
* personal branding or self-promotion
* combination with unrelated symbols or ideologies

Misuse constitutes a breach of conduct.

---

## No Waiver of Rights

Failure to enforce restrictions in a given instance does not constitute a waiver of rights or permission for future use.

---

## Relationship to Civil Trademark Law

This document does not assert registration or statutory trademark status unless explicitly stated elsewhere.

It serves as a declaration of usage policy and intent, not as a legal claim beyond applicable law.

---

## Enforcement

Concerns regarding misuse should be reported according to the Security Policy. The Order prefers correction and clarification over confrontation.

---

## Principle of Restraint

The value of symbols lies in disciplined use. Protection exists to preserve meaning, not to accumulate power.
