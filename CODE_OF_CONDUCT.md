# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes standards of behavior for all participants engaging with the **Order of the Three Nails**, including members, contributors, observers, and visitors to its public spaces and repositories.

The purpose of this document is to preserve dignity, clarity, and restraint in all interactions associated with the Order.

---

## Core Principles

All conduct connected to the Order is governed by the following principles:

* **Respect** — for persons, conscience, and lawful difference
* **Integrity** — honesty in word, action, and representation
* **Restraint** — avoidance of excess, coercion, or intimidation
* **Responsibility** — accountability for one’s actions and speech

These principles apply regardless of rank, role, or seniority.

---

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

* communicate truthfully and plainly
* treat others with courtesy and patience
* accept correction without hostility
* respect boundaries of authority and scope
* refrain from personal attacks or harassment

Disagreement is permitted; contempt is not.

---

## Prohibited Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable:

* harassment, intimidation, or abuse
* discrimination based on race, ethnicity, sex, religion, or origin
* misrepresentation of the Order or its authority
* use of symbols or titles for personal gain
* threats, coercion, or incitement to harm

Violations undermine the credibility and discipline of the Order.

---

## Use of Symbols and Titles

Symbols, insignia, and titles of the Order may not be used to:

* claim authority outside the Order
* intimidate or influence others
* promote political or commercial interests

Misuse of symbols constitutes a serious breach of conduct.

---

## Reporting Concerns

Concerns regarding conduct may be reported to the appropriate officers of the Order or repository maintainers. Reports are to be handled with discretion and fairness.

Retaliation against those who raise concerns in good faith is prohibited.

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this Code may result in:

* warning or correction
* restriction of participation
* suspension or removal from membership or contribution

Actions taken will be proportionate to the violation and guided by the Rule of the Order.

---

## Scope

This Code applies to:

* internal communications of the Order
* public representations and statements
* repositories, forums, and events associated with the Order

---

## Commitment

By engaging with the Order or its repositories, participants affirm their willingness to abide by this Code of Conduct.

Conduct reflects discipline; discipline preserves trust.
