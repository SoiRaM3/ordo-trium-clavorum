# Genealogy and Symbolic Lineage

## Purpose of This Document

This document clarifies how the **Order of the Three Nails** understands genealogy and lineage. It exists to preserve historical memory while preventing misunderstanding, exaggeration, or misuse of ancestry.

Lineage within the Order is **symbolic and moral**, not juridical or authoritative.

---

## Genealogy as Memory

The Order acknowledges that its founder and certain members descend from identifiable historical families and clerical lines. These genealogies are recorded as matters of **historical fact and personal memory**.

Genealogy is preserved for:

* historical accuracy
* cultural continuity
* responsibility toward the past

It is not preserved to assert rank, privilege, or inherited right.

---

## Nordic Ancestry and Clerical Heritage

The Order recognizes **Nordic ancestry** and **Lutheran clerical heritage** as part of its historical background. These elements are acknowledged descriptively, not prescriptively.

They signify:

* cultural formation
* inherited moral expectation
* continuity of vocation across generations

They do not confer authority within the Order or beyond it.

---

## Symbolic Continuity

Lineage is understood symbolically as a chain of responsibility rather than a chain of command. The past binds the present only by example, not by mandate.

Symbols associated with ancestry may be preserved where they serve clarity and restraint. They may not be invoked to legitimize claims of power, precedence, or superiority.

---

## Relationship to Heraldry

Certain heraldic motifs, including the image of **three nails**, appear both in historical family arms and in the insignia of the Order. This resemblance reflects symbolic continuity, not heraldic inheritance.

The Order claims **no family arms** as its own, and no family may claim the arms of the Order as personal property.

---

## Limits of Lineage

Lineage within the Order does not imply:

* transmission of office
* succession of authority
* hereditary membership
* spiritual privilege
* moral exemption

Membership and responsibility arise solely from voluntary commitment and observed discipline.

---

## Recording of Genealogy

Genealogical information, where recorded, is maintained as historical documentation only. It is subject to correction, revision, and scholarly restraint.

The Order rejects speculative genealogy, mythic reconstruction, and symbolic inflation.

---

## Aim of Lineage Awareness

The purpose of acknowledging lineage is to cultivate humility and continuity of conscience. To know one’s predecessors is to know one’s limits.

Lineage therefore serves discipline, not entitlement.
